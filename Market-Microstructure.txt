###Matching Rules - Order Book
Price & time priority is a matching rule used in electronic order books;
1] Price-Priority ( First Rule)
  -Better Price Executed first
  -Higher bid first(for buyers)
  -Lower ask first(for sellers)
2] Time Priority(second rule)
  -if two orders have the same price
  -The one entered earlier gets executed first.

###Types of orders
Market order, Limit order, stop order, stop-limit order,iceberg order,All or Kill[FOK], Immediate or cancel[IOC],Good Till Cancelled[GTC], Day orders, post-only order.


-> An ICEBERG order consists of a visible "peak" that is displayed on the order book and a hidden reserve quantity. Once the displayed peak of the ICEBERG executed, it is refreshed
    with the part of the hidden quantity. This process repeats untile the order fully executed or cancelled.
->FILL OR KILL[FOK] is used when you absolutely have to get the full quantity of an order filled at specific price.
->IMMEDIATE OR CANCEL ORDER[IOC]: very similar to FOK. If differs from FOK, in that if only a portion of volume can be executed it will be.
   IOC order can be fully filled a just partially filled and the balance will be cancelled.
   FOK must be fully filled it will be killed.

###LIMIT ORDER BOOK 
  ->Tick Size: Smallest possible price increment b/w two consecutive prices. Ex nifty=0.05
  ->Minimum Order Size(Lot Size): Smallest quantity of instruments that can be traded.
  ->Ask Side: All active sell orders in the order book
  ->Mid Price: Mt=(at+bt)/2 => (Best Bid+Best Ask)/2
  ->Bid-Ask Spread: St=(at-bt) (Best Ask - Best Bid)
  ->Depth(at price level ppp): Total aggregate order Quantity available at price p

###  LEVEL - I, LEVEL - II  and LEVEL - III data:
        =>LEVEL I -- Top of Book
            ->Diplays best bid & best ask
            -> Shows corresponding aggregate sizes
            -> Alos includes last traded price and volume
            -> Basic view of the "inside market"

        =>LEVEL II -- Market Depth
            ->Shows multiple price levels on both bid and ask sides
            ->Displays aggregated quantity at each price level
            ->Provides visibility into order book depth
            ->used for short-term trading and liquidity analysis

        =>LEVEL III - Order Level Data:
            ->Shows individual orders(not just aggregated levels)
            ->Include order ID, Timestamps, modifications, executions, and cancellations
            ->Provides full order life cycle information.
            -> Typical Availbale to:
                  -Exchange Members
                  -Brokers
                  -Market Makers
LEVEL III data typically includes all trades, fills, adds, cancels, modifies(or replaces) book clear events and depending on the venue and dataset, other special order Events

BENEFITS:
1]More Accurate Passive Order Simulation
  -L3 data provides order-level details, including individual order Ids and Sizes
  -This allows precise simulation of queue position and fill probability
  -As a result, backtesting engines become significantly more realisitic
  -In contrast, with only L2 data, traders must estimate queue position using moels, which introduces approximation error
2] Not only for HIGH-FREQUENCY Traders
  -A common misconception is that L3 data only benefits low-latency or HFT Participants
  -Even non-latency-sensitive traders benefit from improved backtesting accuracy and execution modelling
3]Access to New Signal Classes:
  -L3 data exposes information that cannot be derived from L1 to L2 feeds
  -for ex, traders can measure how long each order has been resting in that book.
  -This enables constraction of signals based on:
    -Order Staleness
    -Order aging behavior
    -Liquidity dynamics at the individual order level
4]Protocol Efficiency and Latency Advantages.
  -Although L3 data contains more granular information across more price levels, L3 feed protocols are often more optimized than L2 protocols
  -This can result in
        -Lower Processing overhead
        -Reduced Latency
  -Counterintuitively, depending on implmentation, L2 data may even require more storage than L3 data.
5]Infrastructure - Based Advantages
  -Some exchanges intentionally distrubute more granuler feeds through faster infrastructure paths.
  -This can provide additional latency advantages to L3 data subscribers.

##### MICROPRICE:
  ->Mid-Price is unrealistic when the volume of limit orders at the best bid and ask prices differ significantly.
  ->In these cases, microprice can be useful. Since microprice is sensitive to change in the orderbook, it may offers some predictive power for future price movements.
  ->The microprice is core high-frequency trading metrice that estimates the "fair" price of an asset by adjusting the mid-price based on order book imbalance, reducing the impact
    of the bid-ask spread. A standard, commonly used formula fo rthe micro-price, utilizing top-of-box(it means L1 data) volumnes
    i.e   Micro Price = Sb.Pa+Sa.Pb/Sa+Sb 
         --Pb & Pa are the best bid & best ask prices
          -- Sb& Sa are the sizes at the best bid & Ask price levels.

#### ORDER BOOK IMBALANCE:    [L2 DATA Needed]
   -> It is a market microstructure metric measuring the difference b/w buy & sell order volume(liquidity) at various price levels in a limit order book.
      It serves as a short term indicator of supply-demand disparity & potential price direction, often used in HIGH-FREQUENCY trading to predict price moves
          IMBn = (Summation(i=1,N)Sbi - Summation(i=1,N)Sai)/ (Summation(i=1,N)Sbi + Summation(i=1,N)Sai)      Note: Only consideres limit orders
      where :
          Sbi is the size at the ith best bid price level
          Sai is the size at the ith best ask price level
          N is the number of levels to aggregate(e.g N=5 for top 5 levels)
          IMBn ranges from -1 to 1

    Book imbalance can look at mulitple levels of the order book.
      +ve values mean there is more cummulatives size in the bid than the offer.
      -ve values mean there is more cumulative size on the offer
      -0 means the bid & offer have the same total size

###### MARKET MAKER
-A market maker, also called a liquidity provers, is a firm or individual that continuously provdies quotes - both bids to buy and offers to sell - for a given financial instruments,
    as primary trading strategy.
-MM generally come up with their bids & offers by calculating a theoritical price for the given financial instrument and then determining how much credit or margin they need to compensate
  for the risk they are taking in providing quotes to the market

##INVENTORY:
  ->It refers to the net position held by the MM in a particular asset
      ->if a MM buys more than they sell ---> Long inventory
      ->if they sell more than they buy ---> Short Inventory
why it matters:
  ->Larger inventory exposes the MM to price risk
  ->MM adjust quotes to rebalance inventory
### INVENTORY SKEW:
    1] Net Long Inventory      2] Net Short Invetory

Model to adjust price & quantity used is:  AVELLENDA-STOIK MODEL  
   Reservation Price = midprice - (gamma)(q)(sq(volatity)(T-t)  
      here gamma = risk, (T-t) time remaining
WHAT is RESERVATION PRICE:
   ->It is "Personalized mid price" accoridng to one's inventory, allowing symmetric quotes around this personalized mid-prie to manage inventory risk.
